NBA’s Mount Rushmore: MJ, Kareem, Magic, LeBron, Shaq, Bird, Kobe?

By: S.Kabir

Mount Rushmore, located in Pennington County, South Dakota, features sculptures of four of the greatest Presidents in U.S history. Some people may argue the Presidents that followed George Washington, Thomas Jefferson, Theodore Roosevelt and Abraham Lincoln may have had a larger impact, but that’s why this analogy works so well with the NBA. Every generation going forward may believe they have seen the best players to pass through the NBA and that the “Mount Rushmore” of the NBA is cemented. However, every now and then, a generational talent emerges and changes the entire landscape of the NBA. Bringing up the conversation of NBA’s greatest of all time (also referred to as G.O.A.T) is a topic of contention, since so many players have come through the NBA in different eras therefore it’s hard to compare. Below is the list of my MT.Rushmore players and why they made my list over some other great players:

Michael Jeffrey Jordan

Key Accomplishments: Six-time NBA Champion, five-time MVP, six-time Finals MVP, 14-time All-Star, 11-time All-NBA selection, nine-time All-D selection, 10-time NBA scoring champion, defensive player of the year (1987–88), rookie of the year (1984–85), Hall of Fame

Career Averages: 30.1 PPG, 6.2 RPG, 5.3 APG, .497 FG%

LeBron James

Key Accomplishments: Three time NBA Champion, Three time Finals MVP, four time MVP, fourteen time All-Star, thirteen time All-NBA Team, six time All-NBA Defense Team, Rookie Of The Year

Career Averages: 27.1 PPG, 7.3 RPG, 7.1 APG, .502 FG%

Magic Johnson

Key Accomplishments: Five time NBA Champion, three time Finals MVP, three time MVP, fifth All time Assists, ten time All NBA Team(1st), twelve time All-Star, Hall of Famer

Career Averages: 19.5PPG, 7.2RPG, 11.2APG(1st), .520 FG%

Kareem Abdul Jabbar

Key Accomplishments: Six time NBA Champion, six time MVP, two time Finals MVP, nineteen time All-Star, fifteen time All-NBA selection, ten time All-Defense Team , Rookie of the Year , Hall of Fame

Career Averages: 24.6 PPG, 11.2 RPG, 2.6 BPG, .559 FG%
